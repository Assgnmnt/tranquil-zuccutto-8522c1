Common questions

Before you apply.

Is Discovery Lab™ the same as the Activation Program™? +
No. Discovery Lab™ is the 4-week foundational activation experience. It helps you name the rung, terminate the agreement, define the mandate, and document the first move. The Activation Program™ is the future 8-week execution and sustainment container for women who are ready to build rhythm, capacity, visibility, and measurable progress around the assignment. Discovery Lab™ comes first.
Do I need to complete anything before applying? +
Yes. The Assignment Readiness Diagnostic™ is required before applying. It is free and takes 3 minutes. It identifies your current stage and ensures Discovery Lab™ is the right next step for where you are. Take it at assignmentroom.com/diagnostic.
What will I have when the four weeks are over? +
You will have a terminated Breach of Agreement™, a finalized Mandate Statement, an Activation Map™, and your Assignment Brief™ PDF — delivered within 48 hours of your final session. These are not worksheets. They are working documents that travel with you into your next season.
What is the investment for Discovery Lab™? +
The public investment for Discovery Lab™ is $1,497 for the full 4-week experience. Founding cohort access is extended by private invitation for women selected to provide feedback and early implementation insight during the initial launch. If you have received a private invitation, your invitation will include your access details.
How much time does this require each week? +
Live sessions run 60–90 minutes per week. Pre-work and reflection between sessions typically requires 2–3 additional hours. This is a building room — it requires consistent presence, not just attendance.
What happens after Discovery Lab™? +
Discovery Lab™ participants who complete the program with a documented Assignment Brief™ and defined mandate are given early access to the Activation Program™ — the future 8-week execution and sustainment container where you build rhythm, capacity, and measurable momentum around your assignment.
