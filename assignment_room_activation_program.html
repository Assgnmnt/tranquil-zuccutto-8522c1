The 8-Week Arc

Four phases. Eight weeks. One sustained assignment.

Each phase of the program has a specific focus, a specific set of weekly sessions, and a specific output. The arc is cumulative - each phase builds on the one before it.

Weeks 1–2
Foundation
01
Rule of Life for Assignment™
Week 1
Audit the current rhythms.
Honest inventory of how time, energy, and attention are currently allocated - and what the assignment is actually competing with. You cannot build a sustainable rhythm on top of an unexamined one.
Week 2
Build the Rule of Life for Assignment™.
You establish the non-negotiables - protected time, boundaries, and rhythms that hold the assignment forward through ordinary seasons, not just inspired ones. The Rule of Life for Assignment™ is produced and committed to by the end of this phase.
Phase Output Rule of Life for Assignment™ - your personal framework of rhythms, protections, and non-negotiables
Weeks 3–4
Visibility
02
Showing Up With the Assignment
Week 3
Define the right visibility posture.
Visibility is not the same as exposure. You define what showing up with this assignment actually looks like in this season - what platform, what presence, what frequency - without building a performance that the assignment cannot sustain.
Week 4
Make the first public move.
You take the first visible action with the assignment - not when you feel ready, but within the structure of this week. Accountability is built into the requirement. This week closes with a declared and completed visibility action.
Phase Output Visibility Plan + first public move completed and documented
Weeks 5–6
Execution
03
Advanced Activation Map™
Week 5
Build the Advanced Activation Map™.
An expanded version of the Activation Map™ - with 60 and 90-day milestones, sequenced deliverables, and a documented decision framework for what to do when progress stalls. This is the execution blueprint for the next season of the assignment.
Week 6
Stress-test the map against your real life.
The map is only as valuable as its ability to hold in ordinary weeks. Week 6 runs your Advanced Activation Map™ through a live scenario - full schedule, competing demands, unexpected interruptions - and adjusts the plan to survive contact with reality.
Phase Output Advanced Activation Map™ - 60/90-day milestones, sequenced deliverables, and a stall protocol
Weeks 7–8
Sustainment
04
Proof of Progress + 30–60 Day Plan
Week 7
Measure what has actually moved.
The Transformation Scorecard™ is built and completed - a documented record of what has shifted across the 8 weeks. Not a performance review. An honest accounting of what has been built, what has held, and what the assignment now requires to mature.
Week 8
Build the 30–60 Day Sustainment Plan.
The program closes with a concrete plan for the next 30 to 60 days - built around the rhythms established in Phase 1, the visibility posture defined in Phase 2, and the milestones mapped in Phase 3. You leave knowing exactly what happens next.
Phase Output Transformation Scorecard™ + 30–60 Day Sustainment Plan

Who This Is For

This program is for a specific season of readiness.

The Activation Program™ is not for women still in definition. It is for women who have defined the assignment and are done letting inconsistency be the ceiling.

The Activation Program™ is for the woman who has the Assignment Brief™ and the Activation Map™ already in hand - and who knows that the gap between what she has documented and what she has actually built is getting harder to justify. She is moving, but not consistently. She is building, but the rhythms are fragile. She is ready to stop surviving her seasons and start building through them.

She does not need more clarity. She does not need another round of definition. She needs a structured 8-week season where the execution infrastructure gets built - with accountability, with milestones, and with the kind of momentum that continues after the program ends.

This is not the right room if -

You have not yet named and documented the assignment. If you are still in the Definition stage, the Assignment Brief™ Intensive or Discovery Lab™ is the right next step. The Activation Program™ builds on what those rooms produce - it does not replace them.

  • You have your Assignment Brief™ and Activation Map™ - and you know the gap between what you documented and what you have actually built is no longer acceptable.
  • You are in the Activation or Sustainment stage - you are moving, but the movement has been inconsistent and the rhythms are fragile.
  • You have tried to sustain the assignment privately and it has not held. You need structure, accountability, and a season of focused execution - not more intention.
  • You are ready to build a Rule of Life around the assignment - protected rhythms that hold the work through ordinary weeks, not just the inspired ones.
  • You want to leave with something that continues working after the program ends - not just a good 8 weeks, but a documented infrastructure the assignment can mature through.
