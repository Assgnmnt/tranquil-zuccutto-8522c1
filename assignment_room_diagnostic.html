The Six Stages

Every result names a stage. Every stage has a path.

The diagnostic identifies which of six stages you are in. Each stage has recognizable patterns, a named barrier, and one clear recommended next step.

Awareness
Something is stirring, but you do not yet have clear language for it. The pull is real - the words are not there yet.
"I know something is calling to me, but I cannot explain it."
Delay
You know enough to move, but something keeps pulling you back. The delay has a name - you just have not found it yet.
"I keep waiting, but deep down I know I am not waiting for more clarity."
Definition
You sense the assignment, but you cannot say it with precision. The work is naming what you are responsible to build in this season.
"I know what it is - I just cannot find the right language yet."
Activation
You have language, but movement has been inconsistent. The work is building the structure that makes progress sustainable.
"I have started. But I keep stopping and starting over."
Sustainment
You are moving, but the rhythms need protection. The work is building consistency so the assignment can mature past the emotion of beginning.
"I am in it, but I need a structure that keeps me moving when life presses back."
Multiplication
You are ready to steward this work beyond yourself - to facilitate, teach, or multiply what has been built responsibly.
"I am ready to bring others into this work, but I need a structure to do it well."

Your result will identify your stage, name the barrier most likely delaying movement, and point you toward the next right room.
