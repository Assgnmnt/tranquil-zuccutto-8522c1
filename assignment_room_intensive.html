What Happens in the Intensive

Five structured steps. One documented outcome.

The Assignment Brief™ Intensive follows the same intentional sequence every time - built to move you from a felt sense of calling to a specific, documented assignment before the session ends.

01
Ladder Audit™
You identify what you have been standing on - and why stepping off has felt risky.
The Ladder Audit™ helps you see the ladder you have been climbing - whether that is competence, recognition, identity, security, or influence - and understand why leaving it has felt costly. This is not an exercise in regret. It is clarity about what the assignment has been competing with.
You stop wondering why moving forward has felt so hard. You see the specific thing that has made staying feel safer than going.
02
Breach of Agreement™
You formally break agreement with the belief that has been delaying you.
Every delay is held in place by an agreement - a belief that has been operating beneath the surface, governing decisions without being named. The Breach of Agreement™ brings that belief into the open and gives you a clear turning point, not just another emotional moment that eventually fades.
You leave this step with a specific agreement identified and a formal decision made. The belief no longer has unnamed authority over your movement.
03
Mandate Statement
You define what you are responsible to build in this season.
The Mandate Statement moves you from broad calling language - "I feel called to teach" or "I want to build something" - to a specific, season-bound responsibility. What you are building. For whom. Why now. Said in language precise enough to explain, pursue, and act on.
You have a statement for the assignment that is specific, clear, and yours. Not a vision board phrase. A mandate.
04
Activation Map™
You turn the assignment into movement.
The Activation Map™ takes the mandate and turns it into milestones, sequenced actions, and a 14-day sprint so the work does not stay theoretical. The map is built around your actual life - not an ideal schedule - so it holds when real life presses back.
You leave with a map that shows you the first moves, the first milestone, and the first 14 days - all in writing.
05
Assignment Brief™ PDF
You leave with the assignment documented.
Before the session closes, everything produced is compiled into your Assignment Brief™ - a personal clarity document that holds your assignment, your barrier, your decision, your mandate, and your first moves in one place. This is the document you come back to. The one you share when you are ready. The one that proves this is no longer only living in your head.
You walk out of the intensive with a document in your hands. The assignment is no longer private. It is named, structured, and ready to move.

Who This Is For

This intensive is for one specific type of readiness.

The Assignment Brief™ Intensive is not for everyone at every stage. Here is how to know if it is the right room for where you are.

The Assignment Brief™ Intensive is for the woman who is done circling the assignment privately and is ready to name it, define it, and leave with it documented. She does not need more time to think. She needs a structure that holds her to the clarity she already has and helps her produce something concrete from it.

She prefers private work. She wants accelerated results. She is not interested in a multi-week group process - she wants to go in, do the work, and walk out with something in her hands. The Intensive is built for exactly that.

She may or may not have taken The Reveal™ first. If she is in the Definition stage and she already has enough clarity to work directly, the Intensive is the right next room regardless.

  • You are in the Definition stage - you know the assignment, but you cannot say it with enough precision to build on it.
  • You have been in delay long enough that you are done waiting for the "right time." You are ready to name the decision and move.
  • You prefer private, focused work over a group setting. You want 1:1 attention, not a shared room.
  • You want to leave with something concrete - not more ideas, not more reflection, not more inspiration. A document. A plan. A named assignment.
  • You are ready to put the assignment in writing - and to be held to what you produce.
